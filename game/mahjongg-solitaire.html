Mahjongg Solitaire

Test your matching skills in this classic tile-matching puzzle game!

Game Overview

Mahjongg Solitaire is a classic puzzle game based on the traditional Chinese game of Mahjong. The objective is simple: match identical tiles to remove them from the board, ultimately clearing all tiles to win the game. This digital version offers all the fun of the traditional game with vibrant visuals and engaging gameplay.

With various layouts to choose from and different difficulty levels, Mahjongg Solitaire offers endless entertainment for puzzle enthusiasts. The relaxing pace and satisfying tile-matching mechanics make it perfect for quick gaming sessions or longer periods of brain-teasing fun.

How to Play

Match Tiles

Click on matching pairs of free tiles to remove them from the board. Only tiles that aren't blocked by other tiles can be selected.

Free Tiles

A tile is considered "free" when its left or right side is open (not touching another tile). Tiles on top of others must be removed first.

Strategy

Plan your moves carefully. Sometimes it's better to leave certain pairs for later to access blocked tiles underneath.

Time Management

Some levels have time limits. Work efficiently to match all tiles before time runs out for bonus points.

Game Features

  • Multiple beautiful layouts and board arrangements
  • Classic Mahjong tiles with clear and colorful designs
  • Hint system to help when you get stuck
  • Shuffle option when no more matches are available
  • Relaxing background music to enhance your puzzle-solving experience
  • Various difficulty levels for both beginners and experienced players
  • Track your progress and try to beat your best times

Tips for Success

Start by removing tiles from the top layers to access those beneath. Focus on clearing tiles that block the most other pieces. Watch for matching pairs that are far apart as they're easy to miss. If you get stuck, use the hint feature to find a match, but try not to rely on it too much as part of the fun is finding matches yourself. Remember that planning ahead is key - sometimes it's better to leave a pair untouched if removing them would block your future options.
